Let $f:\mathbb{Z}\to \mathbb{R}$ be a [[mapping]]. Then it is [[non-negative definite]], iff the matrix:
$$
M = \begin{pmatrix}
f(0) & \dots  & \dots & f(n) \\
f(1)  & f(0)  & \dots & f(n-1) \\
f(2)  & \dots & \ddots  & f(n-2) \\
f(n)  & \dots & \dots & f(0)
\end{pmatrix}
$$
Is [[non-negative definite]] for all $n\in \mathbb{N}$.
